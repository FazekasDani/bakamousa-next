TensionScope is not a traditional sentiment analysis tool. While conventional approaches measure "positive" and "negative" reactions, TensionScope identifies the deep psychological frictions — the Tensions — that actually drive consumer decision-making.

Grounded in Social Identity Theory, Self-Determination Theory, and Ontological Security, TensionScope maps social media discourse against seven core human needs that shape perception, choice, and loyalty.

## The Seven Pillars of Tension

Our framework identifies the precise poles where consumers feel psychologically "stuck," enabling brands to resolve underlying conflicts rather than react to surface sentiment.

- **Autonomy:** Freedom vs. Control
- **Belonging:** Inclusion vs. Exclusion
- **Esteem:** Recognition vs. Humility
- **Competence:** Mastery vs. Frustration
- **Security:** Safety vs. Risk
- **Novelty:** Change vs. Demise
- **Credibility:** Trust vs. Skepticism

## How It Works: The Hierarchy of Interpretation

TensionScope applies a rigorous three-tier analytical architecture designed to preserve interpretive depth while ensuring data integrity:

### 1. Verbatim Matching

Identification of explicit semantic buckets through direct keyword capture.

### 2. Synonym & Linguistic Mapping

Expansion into alternative phrasings, cultural nuances, and contextual variants.

### 3. Qualitative Interpretation

Advanced analysis of intended meaning to capture the phenomenology of consumer experience — how people feel, interpret, and narrate their world.

## Beyond Data: From Signal to Commercial Intelligence

TensionScope does not deliver charts. It delivers **Dimensions of Relevance**.

By clustering tensions with specific content domains (e.g., sustainability, pricing, UI/UX, service experience), we reveal:

- **Innovation Potential** — unmet needs where frustration signals opportunity
- **Threat Assessment** — the scale and intensity of unresolved negative experience
- **Brand Positioning Intelligence** — how your brand and competitors occupy the tension landscape
